Google Trips Plus

See more. Argue less.

Project details

Duration: 1 week during the intensive 3 week UX Engineering course

Group: 5 people

Used tools

Lean Canvas, User Story Mapping

Testing through poster sessions, A/B testing, Think aloud

Microinteractions

Figma, Illustrator

Requirement (purpose?)

Create a minimum viable prototype for a system that in some way act, or are perceived to act, "intelligently" by its users.

Brainstorming result

The Idea

Improve the Google Trips system by adding an intelligent feature for group management.

Why this idea?

Context

Organizing a group trip is a complex problem, because different people have different interests, which leads to misunderstandings within the group. During group travelling, it is not easy to find the attractions which fit everyone’s taste. There is already an app (Google trips) that is collecting data and giving recommendations based on that, but they cannot manage groups, just individuals.

Solution

Solution

An adaptive system which is connected to Google Trips and in this way with Google accounts. Every member who accepts the invitation will be analyzed and the recommended places will be based on clustering of individual preferences.

Process

1st step

We started out by creating the Lean Canvas of the idea, focusing on the problem, customer segment, unique value proposition and solution.

2nd step

Definition of the user goals, activities and tasks using User Story Mapping. The USM was intensively tested with users and refined using the received feedback.

3rd step

Validation of the problem through a survey concerning travelling habits and multiple poster sessions.

We started with a slightly different idea of getting personalised recommendations based on travelling history, liked pictures, pages, people on social media. After the first poster session we pivoted the concept because the audience was not convinced.

4th step

Development of Mockups (using Figma) & Validation

As the project timeframe was 1 week, we skipped the low fidelity wireframes and created high fidelity for faster development.

A/B testing of the attraction scoring.

We A/B tested how the score of each attraction was displayed. In the first two cases users did not understand what the score was about, therefore the third option was created and successfully validated.

Also, while testing, we realised that the “Add to day plan” was not intuitive. In the beginning the bookmark icon from the original Google Trips was used, but people did not understand that it adds the attraction to the day trip, they thought it saves it for later. Thus, the icons were changed as it can be seen in the final design of the screen.

A/B testing of the attraction scoring.

Another A/B testing was done on the “Select next activity” screen that is shown when the schedule is not in time. For the B version, people were confused about what is happening and it took them longer to figure out what the screen wants from them. Therefore, the A version was chosen

5th step

Development of Microinteractions

The final touch was to design the microinteractions system.

Microinteraction

There was a significant increase in the users understanding of the flow after the microinteractions were added.

Remember for next time

Learnings

Starting a project from scratch requires a lot of effort, especially in the beginning of the project when the requirements are set. User testing of the user story map was very relevant because some of the activities we thought are important in the beginning proved to be not needed by the users.
